Core rules for Ixon.

Ixon is a competitive tabletop skirmish game set in a fractured galaxy shaped by war, ideology, and the control of Shard resources. Players command individual miniatures led by a single Warlord, combining lethal dice-based combat with tactical card play.

I. Core Game Structure

Basic format

Players

Ixon is designed for 2–4 players in standard competitive play. Larger games are possible with bigger tables, more Shard Nodes, and adjusted model caps.

Board Size

Standard play uses at least a 36" x 36" tabletop. Larger maps can support longer, more complex games with more terrain and multi-level platforms.

Models

Units activate individually. There are no squad activations. Each model is represented by its own unit card.

Starting the game: Each player rolls a D20. The highest roll goes first, and turns proceed clockwise. Players deploy models in a zone along any edge or corner of the map, starting at least 10" away from each other’s deployment zones.

Spec. Unit

MR: Movement Range

Determines how far a unit can move. Movement is measured freely in inches with no grid or facing.

AP: Attack Power

The number of D6 rolled when the model attacks. Higher AP means more attack dice.

AR: Attack Range

The maximum distance required to initiate an attack against an enemy model.

DR: Defense Rating

The number of d6 rolled when defending against attacks. Successful defense dice mitigate incoming damage.

HP: Health Points

A model reduced to 0 HP is destroyed and removed from the board immediately.

Abilities

Some units include active or passive abilities that modify actions, movement, attacks, defenses, or interactions.

Warlord cards: Warlords include Command Points and Influence values. CP powers unit activations and tactical cards during play. Influence limits army construction before the game.
III. Victory Conditions

How to win

Total Elimination

A player wins immediately when all enemy models are destroyed.

Shard Dominion

A player wins by controlling all Shard Nodes for a full round without that control being interrupted.

Shard Count

At least one Shard Node is required. Standard play is recommended with 1–5 Shard Nodes on the board.

IV. Shard Nodes

Claiming and contesting objectives

Claim

A model may use an action to claim a Shard Node within 1" if no opponent models are within that range and the node is not already controlled by that player.

Control

If successful, the player controls the node as long as their model remains within range.

Command Bonus

While holding a Shard Node, the player’s Warlord gains 1 extra Command Point.

Contested Nodes

If an opponent model enters range of a claimed node, the node becomes contested and provides no bonuses.

No Double Claim

A player may not claim a Shard Node they already control.

Objective Pressure

Shard Nodes force players to fight over territory instead of relying only on elimination.

V. Turn Structure

The cycle of play

1. Draw Phase

The active player may draw one card if they have fewer than five cards in hand. A player with five cards may not draw unless they discard first.

2. Command Phase

Spend Command Points to activate units. One CP activates one unit. Tactical Cards may also be played during this phase.

3. Activation Phase

An activated unit may perform up to two actions. Common actions include Move, Attack, Claim, and Ability.

4. End Phase

The active player ends their turn. Play passes to the next player in clockwise order.

CP Exhaustion

When a player spends all available Command Points, their turn ends after the final activation resolves.

Once Per Turn

Units activate individually and may only activate once per turn.

Resolution timing: Actions resolve after they have been physically completed unless interrupted by a Counter or ability. Counters resolve before the interrupted action’s final outcome is applied.
VI. Combat & Mitigation

Lethal dice resolution

Attack Roll

The attacking model rolls a number of D6 equal to its Attack Power (AP).

1–3: Miss

The attack die misses and deals no damage.

4–5: Hit

Each hit deals one damage before mitigation.

6: Critical Hit

A critical hit deals damage that bypasses one form of mitigation.

Defense Roll

The defending model rolls D6 equal to its Defense Rating (DR). Each die result of 4–6 mitigates one incoming hit.

Critical Mitigation

Critical damage may be mitigated only by using extra unassigned successful defense dice. Two successful defense dice can counter one critical hit.

Deflection damage: Any unassigned successful defense dice may be used to deal Deflection damage back to the attacking model if it cannot be assigned to incoming damage, or unless a card or ability says otherwise.
VII. Tactical Card System

Decks, hands, and card timing

Deck Size

Each player uses one tactical deck containing 20–40 cards.

Starting Hand

Each player draws three cards at the start of the game.

Hand Limit

The hand limit is five cards. Players may optionally draw one card at the beginning of their turn if below the limit.

Enhancements

Played during your Command Phase on your turn to improve stats, attacks, abilities, or other unit performance.

Mobilization

Played during your Command Phase on your turn to affect movement, positioning, tempo, or board control.

Counters

Reactive cards played at any time in response to any game progression.

Card cost: To play a Tactical Card, the player must spend 1 CP play the card from their hand when its appropriate, resolve its rules or functions and then place it in a discard pile unless otherwise specified. A deck or hand may include no more than four copies of the same card when built.
VIII. Army Construction

Warlord authority and legal bands

One Warlord

Each army must include exactly one Warlord. Warlords are unique and may only appear once in a Band.

Influence

Influence is used only during army construction. The total Influence cost of all units may not exceed the Warlord’s Influence value.

Duplicate Units

Basic unit cards are non-unique and may be duplicated unless specified otherwise.

Unique Cards

Unique cards cannot share the same name in a Band, even if they are different variations of that character.

Coalition Keys

Coalition Keys represent legal reasons for cooperation. They do not grant bonuses and exist only for army construction.

Veto Flags

Veto Flags are absolute incompatibilities. A VETO negates any card characteristic/s and overrides all other permissions.
