Warband Construction

Legal Warband checklist

1. Choose One Warlord

Every Warband must include exactly one Warlord. The Warlord defines the Band’s legal unit options and command economy.

Required

2. Check Influence

Add the Influence costs of all non-Warlord units. The total may not exceed the Warlord’s Influence value.

Construction Only

3. Match Coalition Keys

A unit must share at least one Coalition Key with the Warlord to be included.

Legality Check

4. Apply Veto Flags

If the Warlord or unit has a Veto Flag that negates a matching Coalition Key, the unit is illegal.

Veto Overrides

5. Check Uniques

Warlords and unique cards may only appear once. Unique variations with the same character name cannot both appear in the same Warband.

Name Locked

6. Review Unit Types

Warbands may include Infantry, Ranged, Heavy, and Spec. units unless a card, scenario, or future format rule limits them.

Coalition System

Faction mixing rules

Survival 🛡

Cooperation based on endurance, necessity, exile, crisis response, or mutual survival.

Conquest ⚔

Cooperation based on expansion, domination, militarism, hierarchy, or aggressive ideology.

Technology ⚙

Cooperation based on machines, industry, cybernetics, engineering, AI, or advanced systems.

Devotion ✷

Cooperation based on faith, oath, duty, spiritual authority, metaphysics, or sacred obligation.

Veto Flags

A Veto Flag is written as VETO: [KEY]. It negates that feature of any card including: Faction, Unit, Specific Characters, Coalition Key etc. and makes incompatible units illegal.

Warlord Authority

The Warlord determines who can be included. Units follow the command logic of their Warlord, not the other way around.

Core principle: Coalition Keys allow lore-appropriate faction mixing. Veto Flags prevent “soup” armies by making certain ideological alliances impossible.
